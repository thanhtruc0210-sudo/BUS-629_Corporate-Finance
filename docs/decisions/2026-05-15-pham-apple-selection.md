# Company Selection Memo: Apple Inc.

## Company Overview

Apple Inc. (NASDAQ: AAPL) is a leading multinational technology company headquartered in Cupertino, California. The company designs, manufactures, and markets consumer electronics, software, and digital services, including the iPhone, iPad, Mac computers, Apple Watch, and services ecosystem (App Store, iCloud, Apple Music). As of May 2026, Apple's market capitalization exceeds $3 trillion USD, making it one of the world's most valuable companies. All financial reporting follows US GAAP standards with fiscal year-end on September 30.

## Selection Rationale

I selected Apple for three reasons aligned with my analytical interests. First, as a finance leader in technology organizations, I want to understand how the world's most successful tech company structures its financial health and capital allocation. Second, Apple's complex business model—combining hardware sales, services revenue, and ecosystem expansion—provides an excellent case study for analyzing profitability drivers and operational efficiency in a mature, innovation-driven enterprise. Third, Apple's global operations and consistent financial strength offer a stable, well-documented subject for mastering ratio analysis across all categories.

## Preliminary Observations

I expect the following analytical hypotheses:

1. **Liquidity Hypothesis:** Apple's current ratio will exceed 1.2x because the company maintains substantial cash reserves and short-term investments to fund operations and shareholder returns, reducing near-term solvency risk despite high capital intensity.

2. **Profitability Hypothesis:** Net profit margin will exceed 25% because Apple's ecosystem creates pricing power and high-margin services revenue that offset hardware commodity pressures, supporting premium valuation multiples.

3. **Efficiency Hypothesis:** Asset turnover will decline or remain flat year-over-year because Apple's growing cash and investment balances increase total assets without proportional revenue growth, typical of mature cash-generative businesses returning capital to shareholders.

## Ratio Categories Preview

Apple's business model aligns with six key ratio categories: (1) **Liquidity** — managing cash conversion cycles across global supply chains; (2) **Profitability** — analyzing operating margins and return on equity; (3) **Leverage** — understanding debt capacity given strong cash generation; (4) **Efficiency** — asset turnover and working capital optimization; (5) **Growth** — revenue and earnings expansion trajectories; (6) **Valuation** — market multiples and intrinsic value assessment.
